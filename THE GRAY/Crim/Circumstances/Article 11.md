**Justifying Circumstances and Circumstances which Exempt from Criminal Liability. The following do not incur any criminal liability:

#### **1. Anyone who acts in defense 
> of HIS PERSON OR RIGHTS
> 
> PROVIDED that the following circumstances concur:**
> 	 1. Unlawful aggression; 
> 	 2. Reasonable necessity of the means employed to prevent or repel it; 
> 	 3. Lack of sufficient provocation on the part of the person defending himself.
#### **2. Anyone who acts in defense** 
>  **of the PERSON or RIGHTS of**
> 	1. his spouse, 
> 	2. ascendants, 
> 	3. descendants, 
> 	4. or legitimate, natural or adopted brothers or sisters, 
> 	5. or of his relatives by affinity in the same degrees, 
> 	6. and those by consanguinity within the fourth civil degree 
> 
> **PROVIDED**
> that the first and second requisites 
> prescribed in the next preceding circumstance are present, A11.1.(1,2)
> 
> **AND THE FURTHER REQUISITE**
> in case the provocation was given by the person attacked, 
> that the one making defense had no part therein.

#### 3. Anyone who acts in defense  
> of the person or rights of a **STRANGER**
> 
> **PROVIDED**
> that the first and second requisites mentioned in the first circumstance of this article are present, A11.1.(1,2)
> 
> **AND THAT THE PERSON DEFENDING**
> be not induced by revenge, resentment, or other evil motive.

#### 4. Any person who, 
> in order to **AVOID AN EVIL OR INJURY**
> does an act which causes damage to another
> 
> **PROVIDED that the following requisites are present:**
> 1. That the evil sought to be avoided actually exists;
> 2. That the injury feared be greater than that done to avoid it;
> 3. That there be no other practical and less harmful means of preventing it.
#### 5. Any person who 
> 1. acts in the fulfillment of a duty 
> 2. or in the lawful exercise of a right or office.

#### 6. Any person who 
> acts in obedience to an order issued by a superior for some lawful purpose.
